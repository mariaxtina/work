Teaspoon of Ink's Coconut Chicken Curry

Esther made this on her blog in October and it was one of our favorites. We eat a lot of chicken, so I'm always looking for ways to keep it interesting. We never got sick of this dish and we had curry for days. So simple, but so delicious.

Check Esther's blog out here: Teaspoon of Ink ! You won't regret it!

The Recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken thighs, cut into 1/2 inch chunks
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1.5 Tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 Tbsp curry powder
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced into half moons
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 (14oz) can full fat coconut milk
  • 1 (14.5oz) can diced tomatoes, low sodium
  • 1 (8oz) can tomato sauce, low sodium
  • Cumin and ground (cayenne) red pepper, optional

Directions

  1. Season chicken with salt and pepper.

  2. Heat oil and curry powder in a medium-large saucepan on medium heat, let sizzle and stir around for about two minutes. Stir in the onions and garlic, cook an additional 3-4 minutes. Add the chicken, stirring to coat the chicken pieces in the curry oil. Reduce the heat to medium-low and allow the chicken pieces to cook through, about 7-10 minutes.

  3. Pour the coconut milk, tomatoes, and tomato sauce into the saucepan, stir to combine. Add cumin and red pepper, if desired. Allow to simmer for 30-40 minutes.

  4. Serve with brown basmati rice and/or naan!
